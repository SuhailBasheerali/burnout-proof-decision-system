# Use Case Examples: Decision Classification Across Zones

## Overview
This document provides realistic use cases showing how different career and personal decisions are classified into the five decision zones based on **growth** and **sustainability** criteria.

---

## Zone Classification Framework

| Zone | Growth | Sustainability | Recommendation |
|------|--------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Execute** | High | High | Pursue boldly - creates value without sacrificing wellbeing |
| **TimeBox** | High | Low | Pursue strategically with time limits - extract value, then exit |
| **Recovery** | Low | Low | Pause current plans - rebuild foundation first |
| **Steady** | Low | High | Maintain sustainably - stable, protective choices |
| **Avoid** | Very Low | Low | Skip it - minimal upside with real downside |

## Use Case 2: TIMEBOX ZONE
**Scenario: Launch a startup while employed full-time**

### Decision Context
- Current: Employed with stable income ($120k/year)
- Opportunity: Co-found SaaS startup with two co-founders
- Question: Can I build this without burning out?

### Growth Factors
- **Equity upside**: Importance 10/10, Strength 8/10
  - Potential 10-100x return if successful
- **Entrepreneurial experience**: Importance 9/10, Strength 9/10
  - Learning compressed into 2-3 years of intensive work
- **Network expansion**: Importance 8/10, Strength 8/10
  - Access to investors, customers, other entrepreneurs

**Growth Score: ~85%** ‚úì High

### Sustainability Factors
- **Time availability**: Importance 10/10, Achievability 4/10
  - Would require 40+ hours/week outside full-time job (unsustainable long-term)
- **Health impact**: Importance 9/10, Achievability 3/10
  - Sleep deprivation, stress, physical decline likely
- **Relationship stability**: Importance 8/10, Achievability 4/10
  - Partner/family needs attention during dual role period

**Sustainability Score: ~37%** ‚úó Low

### Classification Result
**TIMEBOX ZONE** ‚è∞
- Pursue with intentional time limits: "I'll do this for 18-24 months maximum"
- Action plan: 
  - Set explicit end date for decision point
  - Define success metrics before committing
  - Plan backup (return to full-time work) if health deteriorates
  - Negotiate flexible schedule with employer if possible
  - Check in quarterly on sustainability metrics

## Use Case 3: RECOVERY ZONE
**Scenario: Accept consulting role while managing health crisis**

### Decision Context
- Current: Dealing with anxiety that's affecting work
- Offer: High-paying consulting gig ($200k/year, 100% travel)
- Timeline: Need income to cover medical expenses

### Growth Factors
- **Income boost**: Importance 10/10, Strength 8/10
  - Pays for treatment but only short-term
- **Prestige**: Importance 4/10, Strength 6/10
  - Respectable brand, but doesn't solve underlying issue
- **Career advancement**: Importance 3/10, Strength 3/10
  - Consulting doesn't build toward personal goals, just pays bills

**Growth Score: ~38%** ‚úó Low

### Sustainability Factors
- **Mental health**: Importance 10/10, Achievability 1/10
  - 100% travel incompatible with therapy/recovery routine
- **Medical continuity**: Importance 10/10, Achievability 2/10
  - Travel schedule disrupts specialist appointments
- **Support network**: Importance 8/10, Achievability 2/10
  - Isolation while managing anxiety is counterproductive

**Sustainability Score: ~17%** ‚úó Very Low

### Classification Result
**RECOVERY ZONE** üè•
- DO NOT take this role - addressing health is prerequisite to any career decision
- Action plan:
  - First: Stabilize mental health (3-6 months focused recovery)
  - Secure financial bridge: unemployment benefits, savings, part-time work
  - Find remote part-time role (lower stress, maintains income)
  - Once stable: Revisit career growth options from stronger position

### Classification Result
**STEADY ZONE** üõ°Ô∏è

## Use Case 5: AVOID ZONE
**Scenario: Join startup with unrealistic expectations and bad culture signals**

### Decision Context
- Current: Stable job at established company
- Offer: Startup junior role at lower pay with founder who's known for conflicts
- Why consider: Friend's startup, equity promise, wants to "help"

### Growth Factors
- **Technical skills**: Importance 7/10, Strength 3/10
  - Startup chaos might teach OR might just be context-switching waste
- **Equity value**: Importance 7/10, Strength 2/10
  - Founder's track record suggests low probability of liquidity event
- **Domain expertise**: Importance 6/10, Strength 2/10
  - Learning subject matter you don't actually care about

**Growth Score: ~24%** ‚úó Very Low

### Sustainability Factors
- **Work environment**: Importance 9/10, Achievability 2/10
  - Founder known for public criticism, high turnover, 70hr/week norm
- **Financial stability**: Importance 8/10, Achievability 3/10
  - Pay cut + equity that likely won't materialize
- **Team Health**: Importance 8/10, Achievability 1/10
  - High burnout, people leaving every 6 months, blame culture

**Sustainability Score: ~20%** ‚úó Very Low

### Classification Result
**AVOID ZONE** ‚ùå
- Do not take this opportunity - minimal upside with substantial downside
- Decision note: "Wanting to help a friend" is not a sufficient career decision
- Action plan:
  - Politely decline, cite specific concerns
  - Help friend source other candidates instead
  - Maintain friendship without joining burning ship
  - If founder addresses culture issues, revisit in 18 months

## How to Use These Examples in Your Decision

1. **Find your zone**: Identify which example most resembles your decision
2. **Assess factors**: Rate growth and sustainability criteria 0-10
3. **Set strategy**: Apply the zone-specific action plan
4. **Review timeline**: Timebox and Recovery decisions need explicit check-in dates
5. **Trust the process**: The system works when you honor the wisdom of each zone

**Key insight**: No zone is "good" or "bad" - each zone has optimal strategies. The burnout-proof framework helps you choose the right strategy for your current situation.
